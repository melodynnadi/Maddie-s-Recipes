Pasta

Pasta isn’t just food—it’s a lifestyle. It’s what you turn to when you’re hungry, sad, happy, or just too lazy to cook anything complicated. Whether it’s drenched in creamy Alfredo, smothered in rich marinara, or tossed in a garlicky olive oil sauce, pasta always understands. It’s the ultimate comfort meal, equally perfect for date nights, late-night cravings, or pretending you’re in Italy while eating straight from the pot. One forkful, and suddenly, life just makes sense again.

prep time

5 min.

cook time

15 min.

instructions

step 1

Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the pasta according to package instructions until al dente (firm but tender). Reserve ¼ cup of pasta water before draining.

step 2

In a large pan over medium heat, melt the butter (or heat olive oil). Add minced garlic and red pepper flakes, sautéing until fragrant (about 1 minute). Pour in the reserved pasta water and stir to create a light sauce.

step 3

Add the drained pasta to the pan and toss to coat in the garlicky butter sauce. Season with salt and black pepper. Sprinkle in Parmesan cheese and toss again until everything is combined. If using, squeeze in lemon juice for a fresh touch.

step 4

Plate the pasta and top with chopped parsley and extra Parmesan. Eat immediately, preferably with an unnecessary amount of garlic bread.

ingredients

8 oz pasta (spaghetti, fettuccine, or your favorite)

2 tbsp butter (or olive oil for a lighter option)

4 cloves garlic (minced)

½ tsp red pepper flakes (optional, for a little heat)

½ tsp salt

½ tsp black pepper

¼ cup pasta water

½ cup Parmesan cheese (grated)

1 tbsp lemon juice (optional)

2 tbsp fresh parsley (chopped, for garnish)

tools

Large pot

Colander

Large skillet or pan

Tongs or pasta spoon

Garlic press or knife

Grater
